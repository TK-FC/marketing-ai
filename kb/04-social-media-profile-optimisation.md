## The principle: cohesive and consistent everywhere

Before driving people to your profiles, set them up for success. One business name, one logo/favicon, one set of colours and fonts — identical across Facebook, Instagram, your website, Google, and every delivery app. Multiple trading names = lost customer trust. People use social profiles like Google: answer every question (hours, menu, how to order, where you are) so they never have to hunt.

## Facebook makeover

Facebook is **not dead organically** — a single well-written connection post for one venue reached ~15,000 accounts, 22.5k views, ~250 likes and 30 comments, unpaid. You just have to use it right.

### Business name
- Same name everywhere (Facebook, Instagram, website, Google, DoorDash, Uber Eats).
- Capitalise the first letters; use correct punctuation. An all-lowercase page name reads as amateur.
- **Single location, no franchise plans:** you *may* add your suburb to the page name for searchability.
- **Planning to franchise:** leave location out — Facebook name changes are painful (lockouts, 30-day waits).
- **Multiple locations, same concept:** run **one** Facebook page with a pinned post listing all locations + hours. One page is less work, builds one audience, and suits a future franchise model.
- **Different business types** (e.g. a cafe and a bottle shop): separate pages — different brand voice, assets and location each deserve their own.

### Profile image
Logo or sub-logo/favicon **only** — never a product photo, a person, or the venue. Big brands do this for a reason (Amazon's smile, McDonald's M). If your logo is detailed/wordy, use your favicon so you're recognisable at thumbnail size. Every story you post then carries that recognisable mark.

### Cover photo = your monthly billboard
Prime real estate. Refresh it monthly with:
- Current promotions or events;
- Your most profitable item;
- A full, buzzing venue (comfort + social proof) if you're community-led;
- Launch announcements: "Now on DoorDash/Uber Eats/Menulog" or "Save up to 30% — order direct at www.yourcafe.com.au".
Design it **centred within the mobile safe zone** so nothing is cropped on phones (dimensions/safe-zone template is shared in the members' group).

### Links
Your website link must be present **and tested** — click it after saving. An agency-run page in the wild kept saying "link in our bio" with *no link anywhere*: no way to book, see a menu, or enquire. Use a Link tree if you have multiple assets (catering, functions, bottle shop, retail) and keep it current.

### Bio
Copy your Brand Positioning Statement into ChatGPT: *"Write an engaging Facebook bio from this brand positioning statement."* It summarises it for you. Done.

### Pinned posts (top of page)
1. **Locations + trading hours** (essential for multi-site).
2. **How to order/book** — show exactly how easy it is (app download, link, phone).
3. **This month's promotions** — a carousel of everything on this month (gimmick days, events, specials).
A visitor arriving from a community-group recommendation should immediately find a reason to visit.

### Facebook audit checklist (do this now, assign a who + when)
- [ ] Business name accurate, consistent, properly capitalised
- [ ] Profile image = clear logo/favicon
- [ ] Cover photo reflects current promos / is inviting
- [ ] Bio says what you offer and who you are
- [ ] Website + booking/order links present and working
- [ ] Trading hours and phone number current (one member discovered his page still said "closed Wednesdays" weeks after reopening — check regularly)
- [ ] Location/hours post and how-to-order post pinned

## Instagram optimisation

### Handle vs account name — the search-engine hack
- **Handle (@username):** your business name. Taken? Add underscores or dots.
- **Account name (the bold name field):** this is **searchable with spaces** — so make it your keywords: **[location] + [what you are]**.

**The live test that proves it:** searching "Nowra bakery" in Instagram's *Accounts* tab returned exactly one local result — the member whose account name contained those words — despite multiple bakeries existing in Nowra. The optimised profile is the *only* one found. Other live examples: an East Brisbane brewery surfacing for "Brisbane brewery"; a cafe ranking #4 for "Brisbane brunch" against hundreds of competitors.

Formats: "North Brisbane Cafe", "Tastiest Kebabs in Sydney", "Canberra's Dog-Friendly Cafe", "Brisbane's Go-To Burgers". You can double up ("Brisbane's Favourite Brewery & Pizza"). People search Instagram when they visit or move to an area — this is how they find you, and almost no hospitality venue does it.

### Bio
Paraphrase your BPS into a **skimmable** bio: short lines, one point per line, emojis as visual signals (📍 for location, ☕ for coffee, 🌱 on its own line for vegan/veg/halal options). Lead with keywords ("The go-to family-friendly brewery, East Brisbane (near the Gabba) 🍕 wood-fired pizzas 🍺 award-winning craft beer").

### The link = your most profitable action
- Cafe with online ordering → link **directly to the ordering page** (Square site, etc.) — not the homepage where the button might be buried or broken.
- Big catering/functions business → catering link first.
- Bottle shop → retail store.
- Multiple venues / multiple assets → Link tree, most profitable action first, **kept up to date** (a January event still sitting there in March says "we're not active").
Shorten the steps. If they want anything else, they'll Google you.

### Address link
Add your storefront address link — it's clickable into Maps. Client analytics show this gets real use (e.g. 122 address-link taps in 30 days): people checking how close you are before deciding.

### Pinned posts
Same trio as Facebook: current promos; trading hours + location; how to order or book (reservation required? walk-in? which platform?).

### Verification
Don't pay for it. It only matters if a near-identical trading name exists or you're franchising and need to be *the* verified one.

## Other platforms — "will this drive revenue?"

That's the only question. Going viral that doesn't move revenue is a hobby. For each platform ask: is my demographic actually here, and do I have an offer that fits?

### TikTok
Great for short engaging video if your audience is Gen Z/millennial — but growing there demands **2–3 videos per day**; it's brutally labour-intensive. The audience skews heavily US (~340m population vs ~27m AU): a million American followers don't buy lunch in your suburb. Worth it if you ship retail/merch worldwide. Lighter-touch option: don't run an account — invite local TikTok food influencers in instead (find them via "[suburb] foodies" hashtags or "top 5 cafes [suburb]" videos, and check their audience analytics are actually local before paying).

### Pinterest
Useful if you publish recipes or sell retail products (house chutney, roasted coffee beans) — and can ship internationally. Never a "no", rarely a priority.

### LinkedIn
**If you offer functions or corporate catering, you need to be here.** This is where the decision makers live. Playbook:
1. Create a profile; upload your function/catering packages (PDF), enquiry email and phone into the profile.
2. Search "[your city] function manager / events coordinator / office administration" and connect with hundreds of the exact job titles that book catering.
3. Reach out: "We love [Company] — if you ever need site catering or coffee for a meeting, we'd love to look after you."
4. LinkedIn paid ads can target specific **job titles** — powerful for catering acquisition.
Don't post burger content here; it's a marketplace for B2B acquisition, not a feed to entertain. Delegate it: ask in your team chat who has LinkedIn experience, set clear expectations and a target list — a tasked team member will do this 10× better than a time-poor owner.

### Snapchat
Only if your target audience *and* your team are very young (Gen Z and under). Otherwise skip.

### Action items
For any platform you add: write down what's needed (profile image ✓, BPS ✓, function pack PDF ✓, dedicated enquiry email/phone ✓), **who** on the team will action it, and by when. You didn't open a venue to do everything yourself — task integral players and let them be great at it.
