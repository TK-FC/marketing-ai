# Foodie Coaches Marketing Knowledge Base — Index & Glossary

**What this is:** A distilled, structured knowledge base built from the transcripts of the Foodie Coaches "Crushing Your Marketing in 2025" Tuesday Teach series (January–March 2025), plus the post-retreat "Contagious Content" class (November 2024), presented by Taylor of Delish Digital. It is designed to power a chatbot that answers hospitality marketing questions (cafés, restaurants, bars, bakeries, breweries, takeaway) using this framework.

**Audience context:** Owner-operators of hospitality venues, primarily in Australia (with US/NZ members). Examples, platforms, and spelling are Australian unless noted.

## Glossary of program terms

- **ACS — Average Customer Spend.** The average amount each guest spends per visit. Many tactics in this program (QR ordering, table talkers, upsell screens, targeting couples/families) exist to lift ACS.
- **BPS — Brand Positioning Statement.** A short paragraph stating who you are, what you offer/serve, who you serve, and what makes you different. The foundation asset reused across Google, Facebook, website and employment packs. (File 02.)
- **EEE posts — Engage, Entertain, Educate.** The first content pillar of the 9-post strategy. (Files 06 & 08.)
- **CC posts — Community & Connection.** The second content pillar: team highlights, customer features, local community involvement. (Files 06 & 08.)
- **Sell/Promote posts.** The third pillar: limited time offers, product features, events and bookings. (Files 06 & 08.)
- **9-post strategy.** Content batching framework: 4 EEE + 3 CC + 2 Sell per batch. Effort split roughly 70% engage / 20% connect / 10% sell. (File 06; taught with a fishing metaphor in file 08.)
- **LTO — Limited Time Offer.** A special available for a defined window ("for the month of March, or until sold out") to create FOMO.
- **Gimmick.** A commercialised holiday or trend (Valentine's Day, National Donut Day, a viral drink) used to build a profitable LTO or event. Not a dirty word in this framework. (File 01.)
- **EGC — Employee Generated Content.** Content created by your team rather than the owner, with guidelines, incentives and controlled account access. (File 01.)
- **UGC — User Generated Content.** Content created by your customers (distinct from EGC). One of the "who is responsible for content" options. (File 08.)
- **Content vault.** A saved-collections folder system on Instagram/Facebook/TikTok (shareable/collaborative with the team member who makes your content) holding lookalike-business content worth recreating. (File 08; how-to resource in Elite Mastermind.)
- **Lookalike competitor analysis (the "pirate" method).** Marketing competitor analysis done on businesses in your exact niche anywhere in the world that are winning online — save what worked for them and recreate it — as opposed to financial competitor analysis on the venue down the road. (File 08.)
- **Photo dump.** A carousel of ~10 candid behind-the-scenes shots from the week; people go looking for themselves and engagement follows. (File 08.)
- **Contra (contra deal).** Exchanging food/drink (instead of cash) with a food blogger or influencer for agreed content deliverables. Always documented in a one-page agreement. (Files 01 & 06.)
- **Saturation campaign.** Concentrating every post in a period on one business goal (e.g. fully booked Mother's Day) until that goal converts. (File 06.)
- **Hero image.** The large banner image at the top of a delivery-app or website profile.
- **Favicon.** The tiny logo shown in a browser tab / next to a website name; also used here to mean the simplest sub-logo version of your brand mark (e.g. just initials).
- **Hamburger menu.** The three-line menu icon on a mobile website; should sit top-right within thumb reach.
- **Bounce rate / dwell time.** Bounce rate reflects visitors leaving your site quickly without engaging — lower is better. Dwell time is how long someone stays consuming your content — longer is better. Both signal value to Google and affect ranking. (Files 05 & 07.)
- **Reach / Impressions / Views.** Reach = unique accounts shown your content. Impressions = times people paused on it (3+ seconds). Views = total times it appeared on screens, including repeats to the same person. (File 07.)
- **COGS.** Cost of goods sold. Referenced when calculating whether promotions and delivery-app discounts actually leave profit.
- **Blue Card.** Queensland's Working with Children Check. Relevant if staff supervise kids' play areas.
- **Elite Mastermind.** The Foodie Coaches members' Facebook group where work is shared and workshopped.
- **Tuesday Teach / pods.** The weekly live training session and the small coaching groups members belong to.

## Editorial notes (important for accurate answers)

1. **Source quality.** These files were distilled from auto-generated video transcripts. Obvious transcription errors were corrected with high confidence (e.g. "Guzman y Gomez", "Pyrmont", "MAFS — Married At First Sight", "Meta Business Suite", "Deliverect", "Menulog", "BookTok", "Onyx Storm"). A few proper nouns (some inspiration account names, niche suppliers) are flagged inline as "as transcribed" and should be verified before quoting as fact.
2. **Privacy.** Individual member and client names from the live calls have been genericised ("a member's bakery", "a brewery client"). Results and details are preserved. Large public brands and publicly cited example venues/accounts are kept.
3. **Time-sensitive content.** Series sessions ran January–March 2025; the Contagious Content class ran November 2024. References such as the potential US TikTok ban (decision expected 19 Jan 2025), the Taylor Swift Australian tour, the Grimace campaign, specific movie releases, Easter/ANZAC trading reminders and named viral trends are period-specific. Treat the *tactic* as evergreen and the *example* as dated.
4. **Coverage gap.** The Week 6 analytics recording appears to cut off partway: the presenter promises a live engagement-rate calculation that is not in the captured audio. The three core metric categories and the Q&A are fully captured. The conceptual definition (engagement relative to audience/reach, tracked monthly) is in file 07; the worked walkthrough is not in this knowledge base.
5. **Duplicates in source.** `9-post-strategy`, `the-process` and `closing-q-a` are sub-cuts of the full `content-that-sells` recording; two copies of the ideal-guests intro existed. All unique content is consolidated here once.
6. **Scope boundary the presenter sets.** Marketing advice in this framework deliberately stops at operations and costing: "don't bring more people in until your foundations (COGS, team capacity, contracts) can deliver." Where the presenter defers to operational coaches (e.g. kids menus, review-handling policy, bookkeeping treatment of content food), the files say so.
7. **File 08 specifics.** Distilled from the November 2024 post-retreat session, which pre-dates the 2025 series. A closing segment promoting the presenter's then-launching agency was excluded as sales content. Where file 08 and file 06 differ on posting cadence (weekly nine vs a batch over a fortnight/month), **file 06 is the current teaching** — both agree purpose beats volume.
